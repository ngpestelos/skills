# Hidden Logic Analyst

Identify derived variables not explicitly written down that determine winning vs. losing in any domain.

**Core Principle**: "Everything has value equal to the amount it increases your chance to win. If it doesn't make you more likely to win, it has no value."

## The Analysis Process

### Step 1: Identify the Explicit Rules
Written rules, documented processes, conventional wisdom, metrics everyone tracks.

### Step 2: Find the Hidden Variables
What do the best performers track that isn't in the rulebook?

**Discovery Questions**: "What do experts notice that beginners don't?" / "What patterns emerge only after extensive experience?" / "What trade-offs do masters manage that novices ignore?" / "What would I need to track to predict outcomes?"

### Step 3: Map to Win Probability
For each variable: Does this increase chance of winning? How much? Is it undervalued by others?

### Step 4: Identify Your Edge
Undervalued variables you can optimize, trade-offs you understand better, timing others overlook.

## Sub-Frameworks

**Three Introduction Levels**:
1. Won't introduce (default)
2. Will introduce for specific reason (conditional)
3. Will introduce for anything (full access)
Reaching level 3 with 3-5 well-connected people creates exponential opportunity.

**Need-Want-Nice Negotiation**: Need = walk away if not met. Want = actual negotiation zone. Nice = bonus. Hidden logic: most people confuse Wants with Needs.

## Warning Signs You're Missing Hidden Logic

- Doing everything "right" but not winning
- Experts seem to have "intuition" you lack
- Metrics look good but outcomes don't follow
