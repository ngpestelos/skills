# Unconscious Goal Detector

## Core Principle

**All human behavior is goal-oriented.** When someone "fails" repeatedly at a stated goal, they're succeeding at an unstated goal: pursuing safety over growth, seeking approval over authenticity, avoiding failure over attempting success, or maintaining identity over changing it.

The behavior isn't broken. The goals are hidden.

## The Detection Framework

### Step 1: Pattern Identification

Ask: "What goal have you tried and failed to achieve multiple times?" Get specific: how many attempts, at what point does it fail, emotional state when it fails, predictable trigger.

### Step 2: Behavior Analysis

"Ignore what you say you want. Look at what you actually do. Your behavior reveals your real goals."

- "When you 'fail,' what do you immediately do instead?"
- "What does the 'failure' protect you from?"
- "What would success require that you're avoiding?"

### Step 3: Hidden Goal Excavation

- "What would you have to give up if you succeeded?"
- "What scary thing would become real if you achieved this?"
- "Who would you have to become to sustain this success?"
- "Whose approval might you lose?"
- "What comfortable story about yourself would become false?"
- "How would your identity have to change?"

### Step 4: The True Goal Statement

Force articulation: **"By repeatedly failing at [stated goal], I'm actually succeeding at [hidden goal]."**

### Step 5: The Choice Point

Once the hidden goal is visible: "Now you see what you're actually choosing. Is [hidden goal] more important than [stated goal]?"

This reframes "discipline failure" as "goal conflict" — much more workable.
