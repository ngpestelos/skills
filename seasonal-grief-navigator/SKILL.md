# Navigating Seasonal Grief Patterns

**Grief has a calendar.** Some sadness returns seasonally, tied to anniversary dates and threshold periods. This isn't pathology — it's how loss integrates into a life's rhythm. The goal is navigation, not elimination.

## Step 1: Recognize the Calendar Pattern

When sadness-despite-success appears, explore timing:
- "Is this time of year typically harder?"
- "When did this pattern start? What happened?"
- Identify threshold dates: anniversaries, "last good" moments, loss dates

## Step 2: Navigate vs Fix Reframing

Shift from "How do I stop this?" to "How do I move through this?" The goal isn't "December without sadness" but "December where you know what's happening, have supports, and don't spiral."

## Step 3: Separate External and Internal Planes

Name the two planes explicitly. Release guilt about "inexplicable" sadness. "You can be grateful for what you've built AND sad about what was lost. These don't contradict — they coexist."

## Step 4: Define Achievable Peace

```
Success metric: "Did I spiral?" not "Did I feel sad?"
Success metric: "Did I recognize the pattern?" not "Am I over it?"
```

**Never offer solutions to eliminate grief** — calendar grief persists for decades; this is integration, not failure. **Distinguish from rumination** — "Am I stuck in a thought loop?" (rumination → action) vs. "Is it December again?" (calendar grief → navigation).
