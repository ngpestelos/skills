## Steps

### Step 1: Identify the Commoditization-Scarcity Inversion

Find inversions: What is everyone rushing to learn (likely commoditizing)? What do people dismiss as "soft" (potentially scarce)? What can AI produce volume of but not judge quality of? If the inversion feels obvious, it's priced in.

### Step 2: Map Competitive Advantage Pillars

| Pillar Type | Resistance Mechanism | Test |
|---|---|---|
| Quality Arbitrage | AI increases output volume; quality judgment stays flat | If AI 10x better, does my advantage grow? |
| Synthesis Moat | AI is domain-siloed; cross-domain connections widen | Does each new domain strengthen the moat? |
| Compounding Infrastructure | Knowledge appreciates combinatorially | Does the asset appreciate with use? |
| Judgment Premium | Execution cost → zero means judgment → infinity | Is this judgment or execution? |

Each pillar needs: specific advantage name, resistance mechanism, supporting evidence, and independence from other pillars.

### Step 3: Personality Alignment Filter

Map traits per pillar against verified assessment data (StrengthsFinder, Big Five, RIASEC — not self-assessed). **Energizing** → keep (durable). **Neutral** → keep with awareness. **Draining** → remove or redesign (will fail under stress).

### Step 4: Diversification + Execution

**Wealth streams**: 3+ from uncorrelated categories. Correlation test: "If tech market crashes / AI displaces knowledge work / local economy weakens, what happens to both?" Same direction = not diversified.

**Execution gates**: Each phase bounded by binary pass/fail criteria with pre-defined contingencies. Gates must be measurable (not "going well"). If a pillar has no supporting evidence, it's aspiration — apply `reality-filter` skill evidence standards.
