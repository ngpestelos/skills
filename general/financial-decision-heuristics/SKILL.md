# Financial Decision Heuristics

Practical, immediately applicable decision-making tools for purchases, investments, career moves, and lifestyle decisions.

## Core Heuristics

### The ATM Test (Anton Kreil)
Your liquid savings is **the only score that tells you if you are right or wrong** about how you're spending your time. Monthly: check all balances, compare to last month/year, ask "Is my time valued correctly?"

**Freedom Milestones**: 1mo (barely surviving) → 3mo (emergency fund) → 6mo (breathing room) → 12mo (significant security) → 24mo (major optionality) → 5yr (approaching freedom) → 25× annual (financial independence, 4% rule).

### Risk Assessment (Three Dimensions)
1. **Downside Exposure**: Worst case? Can I survive it? Capped or unlimited?
2. **Upside Potential**: Best case? Ceiling on returns? Can it compound?
3. **Probability Distribution**: Most likely outcomes? Significant tail events?

**Goal**: Limited downside, unlimited upside. Favorable: start business with savings, learn high-value skill, build portfolio. Unfavorable: all-in single stock, large mortgage on salary, loyalty to one employer.

### Assets vs Liabilities (Cash Flow Definition)
- **Asset**: Puts money in your pocket over time
- **Liability**: Takes money out of your pocket over time
- Your $500k home with mortgage = liability. A $200k rental netting $500/mo = asset.

### The Acting Dead Test (Bruce Sterling/Venkatesh Rao)
"If your dead great-grandfather can do it better than you, you're acting dead." Symptoms: spending hours to save dollars, DIY tasks cheaply outsourceable, avoiding spending where it generates returns, hairshirt frugality as life purpose.

### The Trading Up Pattern (Bruce Sterling/Venkatesh Rao)
"If you use it a lot every day, spend the money and get the good stuff." **Trade up**: bed, chair, kitchen knife, keyboard/monitors, shoes. **Trade down/skip**: rarely-used appliances, occasional-wear clothing, guest room furniture, status symbols.

### Time ROI (Codie Sanchez)
"Always ROI for time. Chase the highest return." Before committing time: learning return? Financial return? Relationship return? Highest-value use? **Sequence matters**: build yourself first (skills, capabilities), then invest in assets.

### Lifestyle Creep Detection
Detection: Did last raise increase savings rate or spending? Living better but saving same %? Peer comparison group shifted upward? **Counter-strategy**: Lock lifestyle baseline, all income above → assets.

### Carrying Cost Heuristic
For every purchase: ongoing cost to own? Opportunity cost of capital? New obligations created? Exit cost? **High carrying**: boats, large homes, luxury vehicles. **Low carrying**: quality tools, books, skills, index funds.
