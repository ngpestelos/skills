# Counterintuitive Analyst

Systematic framework for questioning intuitive decisions and popular wisdom, distinguishing genuine insight from random contrarianism.

**Quick check (2 min)**: (1) What would most people do? (2) What's the hidden assumption? (3) What would happen if I did the opposite?

## The Analysis Framework

### Step 1: Identify the Intuitive Position
What would most people do? What does conventional wisdom dictate? What feels "safe" or "normal"? Document: the conventional view, why it seems right, who holds it.

### Step 2: Question the Intuition

| Check | Question |
|-------|----------|
| Historical | When has this conventional wisdom been wrong before? |
| Incentive | Who benefits from this being the accepted view? |
| Selection bias | Am I only seeing success stories? |
| Survivorship bias | Are these examples representative? |
| Cultural lag | Is this wisdom still valid, or have conditions changed? |
| Historical precedent | When has this exact intuition led to overextension or collapse? |

Key questions: "What if everyone did this?", "What's the opportunity in the opposite?", "What's the hidden cost of following the crowd?"

### Step 3: Evaluate the Contrarian Position

| Type | Value |
|------|-------|
| Random contrarianism (disagreeing to be different) | Low |
| Reflexive contrarianism (automatically opposing) | Low |
| Informed contrarianism (evidence-based disagreement) | High |
| Strategic contrarianism (crowds predictably wrong) | High |

Test: Is there evidence? What would need to be true? Who has succeeded this way? What's the downside if wrong?

### Step 4: Strategic Application

| Scenario | Why Counterintuition Helps |
|----------|---------------------------|
| Crowded trades | Opportunity may be priced in |
| Cultural trends | Trends overshoot before correcting |
| Fear-based decisions | Fear often points toward opportunity |
| Comfort-based decisions | Comfort often points toward stagnation |
| Unanimous agreement | Groupthink often precedes error |

**Don't apply when**: Convention is based on solid evidence, contrarian position has no logical basis, being contrarian just for ego, crowd follows Lindy-tested wisdom.

## Key Rules

- Evidence-based contrarianism, not reflexive disagreement — hold contrarian views to same scrutiny as conventional
- Respect the Lindy effect — time-tested wisdom often has validity
- "Popular therefore wrong" is as lazy as "popular therefore right"
- The skill is knowing WHEN the crowd is right and when it's wrong
