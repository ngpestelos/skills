# Risk-First Methodology

**Core shift**: "How much can I make?" → "How much am I willing to lose?" | "This will happen" → "If X happens, I will do Y"

## Part 1: Risk-First Analysis

### Phase 1: Define Risk (Before Anything Else)
1. What is the worst-case scenario? (actual catastrophic, not "unlikely")
2. Maximum I'm willing to lose? (1-2% of total capital per opportunity)
3. At what point will I admit I was wrong? (specific observable trigger)
4. Can I psychologically handle this loss?
5. Is risk/reward minimum 2:1? If not, pass.

### Phase 2: Size from Risk
**Formula**: Position Size = (Maximum Loss) / (Distance to Stop)

- **Trading**: $100K account, 1% risk = $1K, entry $50, stop $48 → 500 shares
- **Career**: Max time: 3 months before reassessing
- **Business**: Max capital: $50K, checkpoint at $25K

### Phase 3: ONLY THEN Consider Reward
What's the upside? Does the R:R ratio justify commitment? Enter at planned price/time. Exit without hesitation if stop triggers.

## Part 2: Preparation Over Prediction

### IF-THEN Template
```
IF [observable condition] THEN [specific action] BECAUSE [reasoning] WITH [risk parameters]
```

### Four Scenarios for Every Decision
- **Bull**: Better than expected → [action]
- **Base**: As planned → [action]
- **Bear**: Worse than expected → [action]
- **Black swan**: Completely unexpected → [action]

## Key Rules

- **Risk definition is NOT optional** — if you can't articulate max loss, you're gambling
- **Size from risk, NOT conviction** — even 99% sure, still risk only 1%
- **Observable triggers only** — "feels bullish" is not a condition
- **Never move stops** — "it'll turn around" = deleting risk management
- **Never oversize from conviction** — conviction doesn't reduce risk
