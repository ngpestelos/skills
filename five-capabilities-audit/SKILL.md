Identify development priorities across five domains where humans remain irreplaceable in the AI era.

## The Five Capabilities

| Capability | Definition | AI Relationship |
|------------|------------|-----------------|
| **Computation** | Mental processing, logical thinking | AI faster, but humans define what to compute |
| **Transformation** | Physical creation from raw materials | AI designs, humans execute in physical world |
| **Variation** | Generating infinite new ideas | AI generates from training data, humans from lived experience |
| **Selection** | Learning from mistakes, finding "good" | AI ranks by metrics, humans determine what "good" means |
| **Attention** | Changing focus, shifting perspective | AI processes everything, humans choose what matters |

**The Loop**: Attention → Variation → Selection → Computation → Transformation → [observe, repeat]

## Audit

### Step 1: Self-Assessment
Rate each capability 1-5 with specific evidence:
- **Computation**: Can you solve complex problems systematically with mental models?
- **Transformation**: Do ideas become tangible outputs through craft or physical execution?
- **Variation**: Do you generate novel ideas grounded in lived experience, not just recombination?
- **Selection**: Can you distinguish good from bad without metrics, and learn from failure?
- **Attention**: Do you choose what to focus on intentionally rather than reactively?

### Step 2: Priority Matrix

| | High Vision Need | Low Vision Need |
|---|---|---|
| **Low Capability** | Urgent development priority | Ignore for now |
| **High Capability** | Leverage and refine | Potential pivot opportunity |

Don't neglect Transformation — it's the most commonly undervalued capability.
