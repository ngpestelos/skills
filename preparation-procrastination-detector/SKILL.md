# Preparation Procrastination Detector

Flag when "getting ready" substitutes for action — endless preparation becomes a comfortable alternative to risking failure.

## The Detection Framework

**Healthy Preparation**: Time spent reduces risk or increases quality of first action
**Procrastination Preparation**: Time spent delays action without proportional benefit

**Rule**: If preparation exceeds 20% of estimated action time, audit for procrastination.

### Riskless Audit

Ask: "Does this preparation involve any risk of failure or rejection?"
- **No** → Likely procrastination — real progress involves risk
- **Yes** → Legitimate preparation

### Warning Signs

| Pattern | Test |
|---------|------|
| Infinite Research | "Could I start with what I know?" |
| Tool Fetishism | Buy equipment instead of using what you have |
| Plan Perfection | Refine plans that could be tested instead |
| Credential Seeking | Get certified instead of practicing |
| Template Obsession | Create perfect systems before doing the work |
| Comfort Zone Disguise | Feels productive but avoids real risk |

### Builder/Seller Pattern (Technical Founders)

A specific variant where real output substitutes for market contact:

**The Pattern:**
- Build: proposals, products, skills, automations, profiles
- Avoid: actual conversations with buyers
- Justify: "I'm productizing," "building the machine," "scaling leverage"
- Result: Infrastructure without market validation

**Red Flags:**
- "I need to build X before I can sell" (skills, website, audience, product)
- 50 proposals submitted, 0 conversations had
- Deflection strategies: "Should I build a YouTube channel?" / "I need an audience first"
- Geography/timezone raised as blocking constraint when async channels exist

**The Test:**
- "What did I build this week?" vs "What conversations did I have?"
- "Am I solving 'how to sell' or 'what to sell' through building?"
- "Would 5 conversations teach me more than this infrastructure?"

**The Prescription:**
- 5 conversations > 50 proposals > 1 YouTube video
- Ask before build. Sell before scale. Learn before systematize.
- If geography is the objection, list 3 async channels that work across timezones.
- If YouTube is the solution, ask: "What would I learn from a video that 5 DMs wouldn't teach me faster?"

### Action Ratio

| Preparation % | Assessment |
|--------------|------------|
| ≤ 20% | Healthy |
| 20-50% | Audit |
| > 50% | Likely procrastination |

## Minimum Viable Action Test

1. "What's the smallest action I could take in the next 5 minutes?"
2. "What would I learn from doing that no amount of preparation would teach?"
3. "What's the worst that could happen if I just started?"

## Key Rules

- Preparation feels productive because it's low-risk — no one can reject your research, your plan can't fail until executed
- Many preparation activities are processes that shouldn't exist (delete before optimize)
- If you can't identify what you'd learn from 5 minutes of action, preparation is probably procrastination
