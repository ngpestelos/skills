## Method 1: Behavioral Evidence (Primary)

Look backward at actions, not forward at aspirations. Mine observable behavior for signals the user's relevance filter would otherwise suppress.

**What to search for:**
- Books re-read or highlighted heavily (292 highlights = enthusiasm, even if quiet)
- Unprompted activities — what they do when procrastinating or unwinding
- Topics that trigger 10-minute explanations when casually asked
- Purchases that weren't "rational" or "necessary"
- What they enjoyed before age 15 (before the relevance filter existed)
- What makes them angry when done badly (hidden investment in that domain)
- Paradoxes or edge cases they've documented without being asked

## Method 2: Conversational Probes (Secondary)

Use when behavioral evidence surfaces a candidate that needs validation.

**Opening** (choose 1-2 per session):
- "What did you read, watch, or explore recently without anyone asking you to?"
- "Where do you find yourself going down rabbit holes?"
- "What would you learn even if it had no career application?"

**Depth Probes** (when something surfaces):
- "What specifically about [topic] draws you?"
- "Do you find yourself going deeper than necessary?"
- "What's the most obscure aspect of this you've explored?"

**Pattern Recognition** (across sessions):
- "I notice you keep returning to [pattern]. What's there?"
- "Several of your interests share [characteristic]. Is that significant?"

## Interest Validation

| Question | Genuine Interest | Obligation |
|----------|-----------------|------------|
| "Why pursue this?" | "I find it fascinating" | "I should know this for my career" |
| "What happens at difficulty?" | "Want to understand better" | "Frustrated, want to stop" |
| "Who asked you to learn?" | "No one — I chose it" | "Expected in my field" |
| "Would you explore if useless?" | "Absolutely" | "Probably not" |
| "How do you feel during?" | "Energized, curious" | "Dutiful, drained" |

**Extra Mile Test**: reading primary sources instead of summaries, learning history and etymology, exploring edge cases, connecting to other domains unprompted, spending personal money on it.

## Output Gate Check

**Before producing any output, scan for these words**: "positioning," "competitive advantage," "market differentiation," "monetize," "knowledge capital," "strategic asset." If present, the exploration has been hijacked by the relevance filter. Reground: "Would you do this if it were useless?"

## Key Principles

1. **Interest precedes skill** — Ask "what fascinates you?" not "what are you good at?"
2. **Depth over breadth** — One genuine deep interest outweighs many surface curiosities
3. **No productivity pressure** — Asking "what's the application?" kills the exploration
4. **Impractical counts** — Interests with no career application deserve the most attention
5. **Suppression is data** — Interests you dismiss or feel guilty about deserve investigation. Ask: disinterest or fear?
6. **Never conflate** interest with aptitude, interest with career, or obligation with interest
7. **Quiet enthusiasm counts** — Enthusiasm that looks like systematic deep-diving (292 highlights, re-reads) is real even if it doesn't look "excited"
