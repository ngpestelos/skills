# Bottleneck Identifier

Find THE single constraint limiting system performance. Improving anything else won't improve overall results.

## Step 1: Define the Goal
No constraint analysis without a clear goal. "What am I trying to achieve? What does success look like? What is measurable?" Without this, you find symptoms, not constraints.

## Step 2: Find THE Bottleneck
Where flow stops or slows most severely:
- "Where does work pile up?"
- "What am I waiting on most often?"
- "If I could magically fix ONE thing, what would have the biggest impact?"

There is always exactly ONE primary constraint. "Multiple constraints" means you haven't found the real one — force-rank by impact.

## Step 3: Exploit the Constraint
Before adding resources, maximize current capacity: remove waste at the constraint, ensure it's never idle, protect it from interruptions.

## Step 4: Subordinate Everything Else
All other elements support the constraint. Don't optimize non-constraints — it doesn't help overall throughput.

## Step 5: Elevate the Constraint
Only after exploiting and subordinating: invest in, add resources to, or redesign the constraint. Then the bottleneck shifts — return to Step 1.

**Watch for sacred cow blindness** — refusing to see constraints in cherished processes or beliefs.
