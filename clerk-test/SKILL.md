# Clerk Test

**Rowan**: Given a letter for Garcia, takes it and delivers it without idiotic questions. **Clerk**: Asked to look up Correggio, responds "Who was he? Which encyclopedia? Where is it?"

## Step 1: Is the objective clear?
- **Yes** → Step 2
- **Genuinely ambiguous** → ONE clarifying question, then act

"What outcome do you want?" is legitimate. "Which encyclopedia?" is clerk behavior.

## Step 2: Question Audit
List every question you're considering. For each:

1. **"Would Rowan ask this?"** — Could you figure it out yourself?
2. **"Am I seeking clarity or avoiding action?"** — Would starting reveal the answer?
3. **"What if I just started?"** — Is the worst case "slight rework"?
4. **"Is this about the objective or my comfort?"** — Objective-critical = legitimate. Comfort-seeking = clerk.

## Step 3: Verdict

0-1 questions before acting = Rowan. 2+ questions before starting = clerk territory. Don't enable question accumulation, perfectionism disguised as thoroughness, or permission-seeking for actions within authority.

*Source: "A Message to Garcia" (Elbert Hubbard, 1899)*
