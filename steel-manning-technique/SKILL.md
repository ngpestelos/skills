# Steel-Manning Technique

**Core Principle**: The opposite of strawmanning. Strengthen the opposing argument — then engage with that stronger version.

---

## Why Steel-Manning Matters

| Without | With |
|---------|------|
| Defeat weak versions | Defeat strong versions or change your mind |
| Confirmation bias unchecked | Forced confrontation with disconfirming evidence |
| Brittle positions | Robust, stress-tested convictions |

**Key Insight**: If you can't articulate an opposing view so well that its proponent would agree with your summary, you don't fully understand the disagreement.

---

## Manual Protocol

### 1. Listen for Understanding
- Suspend judgment until you fully grasp their position
- Identify their strongest evidence and acknowledged limitations

### 2. Clarify Gaps
- *"Help me understand — what's the strongest evidence supporting this?"*
- *"Under what conditions would this fail?"*

### 3. Summarize Fairly
- *"If I understand correctly, you're arguing that..."*
- Include strongest points, preserve nuance

### 4. Confirm Accuracy
- *"Is that a fair representation?"*
- *"What am I missing?"*
- Adjust until they agree

### 5. Address the Steel-Manned Version
- Counter their actual strongest points
- Acknowledge valid aspects
- Identify genuine trade-offs

---

## LLM-Assisted Protocol

For complex decisions or adversarial pressure-testing.

**Phase 1: Build Your Case**
- Use LLM to develop your strongest argument
- Iterate until it feels "bulletproof"

**Phase 2: The Opposite Prompt**
- *"Now argue the opposite with equal rigor"*
- *"How would a skeptic dismantle this?"*

**Phase 3: Synthesize**
- Address the weaknesses identified
- Decide: hold, modify, or pivot

**Why This Works**: *"The model has no position. It has infinite positions. It will argue any direction with equal competence."* — The model that perfected your argument knows exactly where it's weakest.

---

## Diagnostic Signals

### You're NOT Steel-Manning When:
- [ ] Summarizing their view in extreme terms they wouldn't recognize
- [ ] Focusing on weakest evidence while ignoring strongest
- [ ] Adding qualifications they didn't state

### You're at Identity Stage (Idea → Ideology → Identity) When:
- Criticism of the idea feels like personal criticism
- **You can't articulate a steel-man of the opposing view**
- Avoiding information that challenges the belief

### Quality Check:
- *"Could I pass an ideological Turing test?"* (argue their position convincingly)

---

## Common Failure Modes

| Failure | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Confirmation Bias | Make the opposite prompt mandatory |
| Time Pressure | Budget adversarial testing upfront |
| Ego Protection | Reframe: finding flaws early is cheap |
| False Charity | Verify summary is fair with the opponent |
