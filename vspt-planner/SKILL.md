# VSPT Planner

Transform overwhelming objectives into immediately actionable plans: Vision → Strategies → Projects → Tactics.

## Core Framework

| Level | Question | Timeframe | Granularity |
|-------|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Vision | What are we trying to achieve? | End state | The destination |
| Strategies | What approaches could achieve it? | Overall method | The path type |
| Projects | What major milestones mark progress? | Weeks/months | The waypoints |
| Tactics | What do I do today? | Hours/days | The first steps |

## The VSPT Process

### Step 1: Clarify the Vision
"What does 'done' look like?" — Specific outcome, observable completion, worth achieving. Output: single clear sentence starting with a verb.

### Step 2: Identify Strategies
"What are the different approaches?" — List 2-4 distinct approaches with trade-offs. Types: Direct (shortest path), Incremental (lower risk), Outsourced (delegate), Hybrid. Select one with rationale.

### Step 3: Define Projects
"What are the major milestones?" — 3-7 discrete phases, each with clear completion criteria. Each takes days/weeks, has a yes/no "is it done?" answer.

### Step 4: Generate Tactics
"What can I do in the next 2 hours?" — 3-5 immediately actionable items, no dependencies. Test: Can you do this without preparation? If not, the preparation is the real tactic.

**START FIRST TACTIC IMMEDIATELY.** VSPT without immediate action is procrastination.

## VSPT Template

```markdown
## Vision
[One sentence: What does "done" look like?]

## Strategy
**Chosen**: [Selected approach]
**Rationale**: [Why this approach]
**Alternatives considered**: [Other options and why not]

## Projects
1. [ ] [Project 1] - [Completion criteria]
2. [ ] [Project 2] - [Completion criteria]
3. [ ] [Project 3] - [Completion criteria]

## Tactics (First Project)
- [ ] [Immediate action 1]
- [ ] [Immediate action 2]
- [ ] [Immediate action 3]

**Start now with**: [First tactic to do immediately]
```

## Key Rules

- Vision must be specific — "Improve X" is not a vision; "Achieve Y by date" is
- Strategy selection is fast — don't deliberate endlessly; pick one and adjust
- Projects are milestones, not tasks — "Build a website" is a project, not a tactic
- Tactics are immediate — if you can't do it in 2 hours, it's not a tactic
- 10% planning, 90% execution — plans are provisional, revise as you learn
